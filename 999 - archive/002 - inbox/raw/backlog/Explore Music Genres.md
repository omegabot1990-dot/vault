# House

<aside> üí° House music is one of the original forms of modern dance music that has a 4/4 beat. Considered to be the descendant of disco music, house music has incorporated a variety of different traditional dance music such as funk, soul, afro-latin beats and much more. - Wiki _[2]_
    
    House music, a genre of electronic dance music, is defined by its repetitive 4/4 rhythms, typically at a tempo of 120 to 130 BPM. Key elements include bass drums on all four beats, off-beat hi-hats, snare drums, claps, or snaps on the second and fourth beats, synthesizer riffs, and deep basslines. Vocals, if present, may be sung, spoken, or sampled. The genre often employs electronic drum machines, particularly models like the Roland TR-808, TR-909, or TR-707, to create its distinctive drum beats. Additional percussion such as claps, shakers, snare drums, or hi-hats is used to add syncopation. Early Chicago house music frequently featured a rhythm riff based on the clave pattern. African or Latin influences can be added through congas, bongos, or metallic percussion. In house music production, drum sounds may be "saturated" for a more aggressive edge. A subgenre, acid house, is particularly known for the squelchy sounds of the Roland TB-303 bass synthesizer. House music's accessibility is partly due to its production on "cheap and consumer-friendly electronic equipment," previously dismissed by traditional musicians. Producers typically rely on sampled instruments instead of live session musicians, focusing on layering drum machine beats, samples, synth basslines, and other elements. Despite this layering, the texture of house music is relatively sparse, emphasizing the lower-pitched bass register over higher-pitched melodies typical of pop songs.
    

### Characteristics of House _[3]_

Many aspects of house music are similar to¬†techno¬†music. However house does have it‚Äôs own set of unique characteristics:

- **Four-on-the-Floor Beat**: Steady rhythm, typically within 110 to 130 BPM range.
- **4/4 Time Signature**: Standard time signature with a pronounced kick drum on each beat.
- **Funk, Soul, and Disco Influences**: Blends elements from these genres for a melodic and soulful sound.
- **Use of Synthesizers**: Extensive utilization of synthesizers for various sound textures.
- **Prominent Bassline**: A distinct and driving bassline as a key feature.
- **Vocals**: Often includes sung or spoken vocals, central to the track.
- **Diverse Sounds**: A variety of sounds such as percussions, synths, non-electronic instruments, and vocals.
- **Less Emphasis on Heavy Kick Drum**: Uses the kick drum more for rhythm and groove, rather than dominance.
- **Lacks Consistent Drive**: Unlike techno, house does not have a continuous driving beat.
- **Equal Presence of Varied Sounds**: Balances percussions, synths, non-electronic instruments, and vocals.
- **Flexibility in Instrumentation**: Does not strictly require electronic instruments; can rely on samples and non-electronic instruments.
- **Tempo Range**: Typically ranges from 115 to 130 BPM, making it one of the slower genres with a 4/4 beat.
- **Subgenre Definition**: Specific synths, beats, bass depth, and overall vibe often define the subgenre within house music.

### Musical Characteristics

- **Tempo:** House music typically features a tempo between 118 and 135 beats per minute (BPM).
- **Rhythm and Beat:** The classic house music beat is created using a drum machine, particularly the Roland TR-808 and TR-909. It's characterized by a 4/4 rhythm with steady kick drums on each beat.
- **Basslines and Synths:** House tracks often include prominent, repetitive basslines and synthesized melodies. Pianos and jazz-like chords are also common.
- **Vocals:** Vocals in house music range from full song structures to looped vocal phrases and include both male and female voices, often with an emphasis on soulful expressions.
- **Production Techniques:** Use of effects like reverb and delay, as well as techniques like filtering and phasing, is common.

- Wiki _[11]_
    
    Techno, a genre of electronic dance music (EDM), is typically produced for continuous DJ sets with tempos ranging from 120 to 150 beats per minute. It commonly features a 4/4 time signature with a repetitive "four on the floor" beat. Artists in this genre frequently use electronic instruments such as drum machines, sequencers, synthesizers, and digital audio workstations. Drum machines from the 1980s, like the Roland TR-808 and TR-909, are highly valued, with software emulations of these retro instruments being popular.
    
    In techno music, the emphasis is primarily on rhythm rather than other musical aspects. The progression of techno tracks often revolves around the manipulation of the timbral characteristics of synthesizer presets. Unlike other EDM forms that are typically produced using synthesizer keyboards and adhere to Western harmonic structures, techno does not always strictly follow these harmonic practices. Instead, it often prioritizes timbral manipulation. A key distinguishing feature of techno music and its aesthetic is the creative embrace of music production technology.
    

### **Characteristics of Techno**

- **Repetitive Four-on-the-Floor Beat**: A continuous, steady rhythm typically in the range of 120 to 150 BPM.
- **Electronic Drums**: Utilization of electronic drum sounds is a key component.
- **Synthesizers**: Widespread use of synthesizers to create distinctive soundscapes.
- **Computer-Generated Sounds**: Incorporation of sounds that are produced digitally.
- **Drum Machines Usage**: Common use of drum machines, especially iconic models like the Roland TR-808 or TR-909.
- **Sequencers**: Employing sequencers to create and arrange the music.
- **Experimental Nature**: A strong emphasis on experimentation with sounds and structure.
- **Abstract Quality**: Techno often has an abstract, non-linear approach to musical composition.
- **Minimalism**: Focus on minimalistic, rhythm-centric structures over melody.
- **Distinct from House Music**: While part of the broader electronic music family, techno is less melodic and soulful than house music, focusing more on rhythmic complexity and electronic elements.

### Musical Characteristics

- **Tempo:** Techno generally has a tempo ranging from 120 to 140/150 beats per minute (BPM), though some subgenres may go beyond this range.
- **Rhythm and Beat:** A distinctive 4/4 beat is a staple of techno. The rhythm typically features a kick drum on every beat, a snare or clap on the second and fourth beats, and open hi-hats on the off-beat.
- **Basslines and Synths:** Repetitive, syncopated bass lines are common. Synthesizers are used to create various sounds and textures.
- **Minimalism and Repetition:** Techno tracks often feature minimalistic patterns, focusing on rhythm and texture over melody and harmony.
- **Looping and Layering:** Tracks usually evolve through the looping and layering of different musical elements, creating a hypnotic effect.

In the spirit of EDM and related forms of electronica, it also incorporates twenty-first-century sampling and synth technology.

Stylistically, nu disco shares many characteristics with French house music, which is also known as disco house. Yet, while French house leans heavily on synthesizers, samplers, and a steady, unchanging groove, nu disco adds in elements from the live disco music of the 1970s.

</aside>

### **Characteristics of Nu Disco**

- **Tempo and Rhythm:** Similar to classic disco, Nu Disco typically features a steady four-to-the-floor beat, with tempos usually ranging from 110 to 125 beats per minute.
- **Instrumentation:** Nu Disco often utilizes traditional disco elements like string sections, horns, electric piano, and funk basslines, combined with modern synthesizers and electronic effects.
- **Production Style:** While retaining the analog warmth of classic disco, Nu Disco incorporates contemporary production techniques, including digital recording and mixing.
- **Vocals:** Vocals in Nu Disco can range from full lyrical songs, reminiscent of traditional disco, to minimalistic and more electronically altered vocals.
- **Aesthetic:** Nu Disco often embraces a retro-futuristic aesthetic, drawing on 1970s and 1980s visual styles while integrating modern electronic music culture.
- **Core funk grooves:** Many nu disco tracks sample core drum grooves from 1970s funk, soul, and disco.
- **Modern synths:** Similar to the more synthetic forms of EDM and electronica, nu disco uses contemporary synth technology.
- **Selected live instrumentation:** In a nod to the original disco movement, nu disco often features bass lines and guitar licks played by live musicians.
- **Long, repetitive grooves:** Nu disco embraces the long run times of 1970s and 1980s disco, but it concerns itself less with the structured sections and pop hooks of those earlier genres.

# Problem Statement

> **Classification of Electronic Dance Music into Genres and Subgenres**

### Background

Electronic Dance Music (EDM) encompasses a wide array of genres and subgenres, each characterized by distinct musical elements such as tempo, rhythm, and instrumentation. The vast diversity and continual evolution of EDM present significant challenges in accurately classifying tracks into their respective genres and subgenres. This classification is crucial for various stakeholders, including music streaming services, artists, producers, and enthusiasts, as it enhances music discovery, recommendation, and archival processes.

### Problem Definition

The primary challenge lies in developing an accurate, efficient, and scalable solution to classify EDM tracks into their specific genres and subgenres. Traditional manual classification methods are subjective and labor-intensive, leading to inconsistencies and inefficiencies. Moreover, the subjective nature of music genres, influenced by cultural, historical, and personal perceptions, adds a layer of complexity to the classification process.

### Specific Challenges

1. **Variety and Evolution of Genres**: EDM is not static; it continuously evolves, with new subgenres emerging. An effective classification system must adapt to these changes.
2. **Subjectivity**: What defines a genre or subgenre can be subjective and vary across different cultures and listeners.
3. **Feature Extraction**: Identifying and extracting relevant features (e.g., beat, tempo, melody, instruments) from complex audio signals that accurately capture the essence of various EDM genres.
4. **Data Availability and Labeling**: Collecting a comprehensive and representative dataset of EDM tracks that are accurately labeled with their genres and subgenres.
5. **Algorithmic Complexity**: Designing and implementing machine learning algorithms capable of handling the nuances and complexities of EDM classification.
6. **Integration with Platforms**: Ensuring the classification system can be effectively integrated into various music platforms and services.

### Objectives

The project aims to:

- Develop an AI/ML-based system to accurately classify EDM tracks into genres and subgenres.
- Create a model that learns from a large dataset of labeled EDM tracks and continuously adapts to new trends and styles.
- Ensure the system is scalable and can be integrated with existing music platforms and databases.

### Expected Outcomes

- A robust classification system that enhances the accuracy and efficiency of EDM categorization.
- Improved user experience on music platforms through better music recommendation and discovery.
- A scalable and adaptable model that evolves with the changing landscape of EDM.

### Significance

Successfully addressing this problem would not only streamline music categorization processes but also significantly enhance the user experience in music consumption and exploration. It holds potential benefits for music industry stakeholders, including artists, record labels, streaming services, and listeners.
