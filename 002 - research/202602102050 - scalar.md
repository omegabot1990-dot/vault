- A scalar is a single number used to scale other objects
- We usually take scalars from the real numbers $\mathbb{R}$

> [!MATH] Scalar ($x$)
> A scalar is an element of $\mathbb{R}$:
> $$x \in \mathbb{R}$$

- Scalar multiplication
	- Multiply a scalar and a vector to scale every component

> [!MATH] Scalar multiplication
> For $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbf{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$:
> $$\alpha\mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix}\alpha v_1 \\ \vdots \\ \alpha v_n\end{bmatrix}$$
