- A <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">scalar</mark> is a <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">single number</mark>
	- Usually a real number, e.g. $x \in \mathbb{R}$
- In linear algebra, scalars are the simplest objects we build vectors and matrices from
- In ML code, a scalar is often stored as a <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">tensor</mark> with <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">shape</mark> `()`

> [!MATH] Scalar
> $x \in \mathbb{R}$
> 
> - You can do the usual arithmetic with scalars: $+$, $-$, $\times$, $\div$, exponentiation
