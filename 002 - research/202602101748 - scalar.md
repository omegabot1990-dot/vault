- A **scalar** is a single real number (a 0th-order tensor)
- We typically write scalars as lowercase letters, e.g. $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$

> [!MATH] Scalar
> $x \in \mathbb{R}$
> 
> - Scalars support the usual arithmetic: $+$, $-$, $\times$, $\div$, exponentiation

- In ML code, a scalar is often represented as a tensor with shape `()`
