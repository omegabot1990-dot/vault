- A vector is an element of $\mathbb{R}^n$
- You can write a vector as a column of components

> [!MATH] Vector ($\mathbf{x}$)
> A vector in $n$ dimensions:
> $$\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n$$
> 
> $$\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 \\ \vdots \\ x_n\end{bmatrix}$$

- Vector addition

> [!MATH] Vector addition
> For $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{y} \in \mathbb{R}^n$:
> $$\mathbf{x} + \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix}x_1 + y_1 \\ \vdots \\ x_n + y_n\end{bmatrix}$$

- Scalar multiplication

> [!MATH] Scalar multiplication
> For $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n$:
> $$\alpha\mathbf{x} = \begin{bmatrix}\alpha x_1 \\ \vdots \\ \alpha x_n\end{bmatrix}$$
