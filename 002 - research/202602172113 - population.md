- In [[202602062059 - statistics|statistics]], a <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">population is the complete set of units or outcomes of interest</mark>
- Population-level quantities are parameters, such as the [[202602062110 - mean|mean]] $\mu$, [[202602062206 - variance|variance]] $\sigma^2$, and proportion $p$
- [[202602111350 - inference|Inference]] aims to use sample information to reason about the population under uncertainty
- For a [[202602061340 - random variable|random variable]] $X$ representing a population distribution:

> [!MATH] Population Mean and Variance
> $$\mu = \mathbb{E}[X], \qquad \sigma^2 = \mathbb{E}\left[(X-\mu)^2\right]$$

- A clear population definition is essential for valid data collection and evaluation
