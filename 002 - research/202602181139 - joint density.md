- The joint density describes how [[202602062040 - probability|probability]] mass is distributed over multiple continuous [[202602061340 - random variable|random variables]] together
- For two variables $X,Y$, the joint density $f_{X,Y}(x,y)$ defines probabilities over regions in the plane
- For region $A\subseteq\mathbb{R}^2$:

> [!MATH] Joint Density Probability
> $$\Pr\big((X,Y)\in A\big)=\iint_A f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dx\,dy$$

- Valid joint densities satisfy:

> [!MATH] Joint Density Probability Rules
> $$f_{X,Y}(x,y)\ge 0$$
> $$\iint_{\mathbb{R}^2} f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dx\,dy=1$$
> If $X$ and $Y$ are independent, then:
>  $$f_{X,Y}(x,y)=f_X(x)f_Y(y)$$

> [!MATH] Marginal Densities
> $$f_X(x)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dy,\qquad f_Y(y)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dx$$
