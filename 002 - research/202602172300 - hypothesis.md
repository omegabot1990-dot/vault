- In statistical inference, a hypothesis is a precise claim about a [[202602172113 - population|population parameter]] or [[202602072325 - distribution|distribution]]
- Hypothesis testing compares a <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">null hypothesis</mark> $H_0$ against an <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">alternative hypothesis</mark> $H_1$
- $H_0$ usually encodes no effect or status quo, while $H_1$ encodes the effect of interest
- Evidence is summarised with a test statistic and a [[202602172258 - p-value|p-value]] under the assumptions of the test model

> [!MATH] Typical Mean Test Setup
> $$H_0:\mu=\mu_0, \qquad H_1:\mu\neq\mu_0$$

- Decision rules often compare the p-value to a significance level $\alpha$
- Statistical decisions should be interpreted with effect size, uncertainty, and assumptions
