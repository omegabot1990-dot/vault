- RoPE encodes position by rotating query and key vectors in paired dimensions using angle frequencies tied to token index
- This makes attention scores depend on relative position offsets while preserving efficient dot-product computation
- Compared with absolute embeddings, RoPE generally improves length extrapolation and long-context behavior
- It is widely used in modern decoder-only LLMs due to strong empirical performance and low overhead
- RoPE variants include scaling and interpolation schemes to extend usable context length
- Performance depends on base frequency settings and long-context adaptation strategy
