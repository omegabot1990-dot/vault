- A [[202602010047 - objective function|loss function]] maps <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">model predictions and targets to a scalar error signal</mark>
- [[202602111335 - training|Training]] minimises expected loss to fit [[202602111314 - model parameters|parameters]] to data
- Different tasks use different losses, such as [[202602120102 - cross-entropy|cross-entropy]] for [[202602120054 - classification|classification]] and MSE/MAE for [[202602120056 - regression|Regression]]
- <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Lower loss usually indicates better fit on the measured dataset</mark>, <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">but not always better generalisation</mark>
- Validation loss is commonly used for [[202602010044 - model|model]] selection and [[202602111733 - early stopping|early stopping]]
- Loss design affects [[202602111605 - optimization algorithms|optimization]] behaviour, gradient scale, and robustness to outliers
