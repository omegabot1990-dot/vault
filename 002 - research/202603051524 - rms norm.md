- RMSNorm scales activations by their root-mean-square value without mean-centering
- For input vector $x\in\mathbb{R}^d$, a common form is:
  - $$\mathrm{RMSNorm}(x)=\gamma \odot \frac{x}{\sqrt{\frac{1}{d}\sum_{i=1}^{d}x_i^2 + \epsilon}}$$
- Unlike LayerNorm, RMSNorm omits subtraction of the mean and only normalizes magnitude
- This reduces computation while keeping stable activation scales during training
- RMSNorm is widely used in modern LLM architectures for efficient and stable optimization
- Performance depends on epsilon choice, parameterization, and placement within residual blocks
