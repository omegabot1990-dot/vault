- RMSNorm scales activations by their root-mean-square value without mean-centring
- For the input vector $x\in\mathbb{R}^d$, a common form is:

> [!MATH] RMS Norm
  $$\mathrm{RMSNorm}(x)=\gamma \odot \frac{x}{\sqrt{\frac{1}{d}\sum_{i=1}^{d}x_i^2 + \epsilon}}$$

- Unlike [[202602191623 - layer normalization|LayerNorm]], RMSNorm omits subtraction of the [[202602062110 - mean|mean]] and only normalizes magnitude
- This reduces computation while keeping stable activation scales during [[202602111335 - training|training]]
- RMSNorm is widely used in modern [[202602191524 - large language model|LLM]] architectures for efficient and stable [[202602111605 - optimization algorithms|optimization]]
- Performance depends on epsilon choice, parameterization, and placement within [[202602191621 - residual connection|residual blocks]]
