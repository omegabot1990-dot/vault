- A <mark style="background: #FF5582A6;">Distribution</mark> is a mathematical function or "map" that tells you all the possible values a [[202602061340 - random variable|random variable]] can take and how frequently (or likely) they occur [^1]
- For discrete data, we use the [[202602091258 - probability mass function|Probability Mass Function]]
- For continuous data, we use the [[202602091301 - probability density function|Probability Density Function]]

- Uniform [^4]
	- Every outcome is equally likely (like a fair die)

![Uniform-CDF|700](https://d2l.ai/_images/output_distributions_c7d568_99_0.svg)

- [[202602072342 - normal distribution|Normal (Gaussian)]] [^5]
	- <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">The "Bell Curve" </mark>
	- Most outcomes are near the middle, and they get rarer as you move away (like human height)

![Normal-PDF|700](https://d2l.ai/_images/output_distributions_c7d568_207_0.svg)

![Normal-CDF|700](https://d2l.ai/_images/output_distributions_c7d568_219_0.svg)

- Bernoulli [^2]
	- A single "yes/no" trial (like a coin flip)

![Bernoulli-PMF|700](https://d2l.ai/_images/output_distributions_c7d568_15_0.svg)

![Bernoulli-CDF|700](https://d2l.ai/_images/output_distributions_c7d568_27_0.svg)

- Poisson [^3]
	- Counts how many times an event happens in a fixed time (like how many emails you get in an hour)
